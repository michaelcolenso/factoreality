# Content Factory Spec

## Product Type
ebook

## Topic & Angle
The IEP & 504 Parent Playbook: How to Get Your Child the Support They're Legally
Entitled To — Without Hiring a $5,000 Advocate

A practical, jargon-free handbook for parents of children with ADHD, autism, or
learning disabilities navigating the US public school special education system.
Covers federal rights under IDEA and Section 504, meeting preparation, scripted
responses for common school pushback, legal timelines schools must follow, and
step-by-step escalation paths when schools stall or deny services.

## Target Audience
- Who: Parents of school-age children (K–12) with ADHD, autism, dyslexia, or
  other learning disabilities enrolled in US public schools; no prior legal
  knowledge assumed
- Already knows: Their child has a diagnosis or suspected learning difference;
  they've likely attended at least one school meeting; they feel lost, steamrolled,
  or ignored in the process
- Needs: Clear explanation of their legal rights, exact language to use in meetings
  and emails, what timelines schools must follow, and how to escalate without
  burning bridges
- Willing to pay: $49–$99 one-time — signals they want a professional, actionable
  resource, not another Facebook thread

## Tone & Voice
- Style: friendly-expert — warm and empathetic to the emotional weight, authoritative
  on legal and procedural facts
- Person: second (you/your child)
- Reading level: grade 8 — accessible to all education levels; legal terms defined
  on first use

## Competitive Landscape
- Existing products:
  1. Wrightslaw "From Emotions to Advocacy" by Pam and Pete Wright ($25 book,
     comprehensive but dense and legal-heavy, requires a second book to fully apply)
  2. IEP Advocate training courses (e.g., Autism Speaks IEP guide, $0–$300,
     scattered and incomplete on escalation)
  3. State DOE parent handbooks (free, official, but written in bureaucratic prose
     with no actionable scripts or templates)
- Differentiation: Combines legal accuracy with scripted, ready-to-use language
  organized by the exact meeting sequence parents face. Includes copy-paste email
  templates and a legal timeline cheat sheet. Priced under $99 — far below the
  $3,000–$10,000 cost of a human advocate.

## Hard Constraints
- Length: 12,000–16,000 words (main ebook)
- Sections: 8–10 chapters
- Formats: PDF (primary), DOCX (secondary)
- Brand: Navy blue (#1a3a5c) and white with gold accent (#f4a820); no logo required

3. Template pack: 5 copy-paste email and meeting request templates (Markdown/DOCX)

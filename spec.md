# Content Factory Spec

## Product Type
resource-guide

## Topic & Angle
The Medical Bill Negotiation & Dispute Kit: a practical, step-by-step self-advocacy system that helps U.S. patients audit bills for errors, dispute invalid charges, negotiate balances down, access charity care/financial assistance, appeal insurance denials, and protect credit from medical collections.

## Target Audience
- Who: U.S.-based adults with large, unexpected, or confusing medical bills; often financially stressed, time-constrained, and unfamiliar with billing/insurance systems.
- Already knows: Basic healthcare terms and that medical bills can be expensive; limited understanding of coding, itemized bills, No Surprises Act rights, and formal appeal workflows.
- Needs: A plain-English, scripted, confidence-building process that tells them exactly what to do, in what order, with what wording and documentation.
- Willing to pay: $39-$97 depending on depth of templates/scripts and “complete defense” scope.

## Tone & Voice
- Style: authoritative + empathetic + action-oriented
- Person: second person
- Reading level: grade 8

## Competitive Landscape
- Existing products:
  - Dollar For (nonprofit support for hospital financial assistance)
  - 1:1 patient advocate services ($150-$500+)
  - Fragmented blog posts and forum advice without an integrated workflow
- Differentiation:
  - End-to-end battle-tested workflow from bill intake to collections defense
  - Exact scripts/letters/checklists instead of generic advice
  - Built for self-advocacy at consumer-friendly price points
  - Includes legal-rights framing (No Surprises Act) and credit-protection actions

## Hard Constraints
- Length: 9,000-12,000 words total core guide (~50-70 pages in formatted PDF)
- Sections: 7 core modules + 10+ tactical templates/assets
- Formats: PDF (primary), DOCX templates, TXT quick scripts
- Brand: clean/high-trust, white/blue palette, high-contrast typography, no fear-mongering claims

## Deliverables
1. 50-70 page PDF: **The Medical Bill Negotiation & Dispute Kit**.
2. Bill Audit Checklist (fillable format + printable PDF).
3. Itemized Bill Request Letter Template (DOCX + plain text).
4. Negotiation Call Scripts Pack (provider, billing office, collections, insurer).
5. Financial Hardship / Charity Care Application Guide + letter template.
6. Insurance Denial Appeal Letter Pack (level 1 + escalation variants).
7. No Surprises Act quick-rights reference sheet.
8. Payment Plan Negotiation script + settlement framework.
9. Medical Debt & Credit Protection quick guide.
10. Collection Agency Validation/Dispute Letter templates.
11. Bonus upsell-ready mini-guides:
    - Insurance Denial Appeal Deep Dive
    - Collection Defense Pack

## Product Architecture (Locked)
1. **Audit First:** How to request itemization, EOBs, and billing records; error-spotting workflow.
2. **Dispute Invalid Charges:** Coding mismatches, duplicate/phantom charges, non-rendered services.
3. **Negotiate Confidently:** Price reduction asks, lump-sum settlement offers, hardship-based concessions.
4. **Apply for Aid:** Charity care, financial assistance, income-document prep, follow-up cadence.
5. **Appeal Insurance Denials:** Internal appeal sequence, evidence packets, escalation language.
6. **Secure Payment Terms:** Interest-free plans, affordability anchors, written confirmation checklist.
7. **Protect Credit & Fight Collections:** Validation, dispute windows, documentation and call logs.

## Go-to-Market Priorities
1. SEO content cluster: “how to negotiate medical bills,” “medical bill too high,” “can’t afford medical bills,” “medical bill errors,” “appeal insurance denial.”
2. Short-form proof content (TikTok/Reels/Shorts): dramatic before→after bill reduction stories with disclaimers.
3. Community distribution: Reddit (r/personalfinance, r/HealthInsurance, r/povertyfinance) and FB support groups with value-first educational posts.
4. Paid search tests on high-intent billing/negotiation queries; retargeting to tiered offers.

## Compliance & Risk Notes
- Include clear disclaimer: educational resource, not legal/medical advice.
- Avoid guaranteed outcome claims; use probability framing and scenario examples.
- Encourage users to verify state-specific protections and insurer plan rules.


## Subagent Orchestration Directives
- Research Analyst: Build a citation ledger for all claims used in the guide and sales materials.
- Content Architect: Ensure each core module ends with a “Do This Now” action block.
- Template Engineer: For every template, include purpose, when to use, how to submit, and follow-up timeline.
- QA Reviewer: Score for actionability, emotional clarity, compliance-safe language, and buyer momentum.
- Compliance Editor: Validate disclaimer placement and remove any guaranteed-outcome language.

## Acceptance Criteria by Buyer Outcome
- Buyer can identify likely billing errors in under 20 minutes using the checklist.
- Buyer can run at least one negotiation call using scripts without additional coaching.
- Buyer can draft and send an appeal/dispute letter in one sitting.
- Buyer can choose a next-best action if insurer/provider refuses initial request.
- Buyer understands credit-protection steps before entering collections calls.
